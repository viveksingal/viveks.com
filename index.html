RUN TO

An Mobile App design to track your runs in Toronto

The Problem

As an avid runner I found out that user interfaces weren’t designed to display the most relevant information. The problem that I was trying to improve was “ How can we display the most important information to users pre run and post run by giving detailed analyzes”

User Research

I than went forward to conduct market research from other running application and performing primary and secondary research. I conducted primary research by talk to avid runners, friends and viewing comments on the Apple store for other running applications.

The research showed that individuals wanted more intuitive dashboard that would provide key metrics such as post run analyses, calories burned, distance run, average pace and total distance run. Also individuals wanted to know how they compared themselves to there previous run in which case we opted into showcase a graph which has been informative.

BrainStation Web Content

The Rebuild

BrainStation is a progressive educational institution that is powering the next generation of creators. Fuelled by Konrad Group, a global leader in the consumer and enterprise technology space. After completing front-end and back-end development BrainStation approached our Cohort and requested us to build a brand new student portal with the help of Educators and members of Konrad Group.

The Design problem that was presented to us “ Students and educators had very basic access to what could be displayed and added to the portal & the user flows, and user journey’s where not structured to be an optimal digital solution”

Teams where broken down into groups of three’s which consisted of Creative team, App logic team, and back-end team. I personally put on the Creative team where we created designs and developed UI of the web application.

There was objects that needed to be meet with this project:

  1. Progress tracking ( Visibility of system status)
  2. Live searching content ( User control and freedom)
  3. Consistency and standards of the BrainStation brand
  4. Aesthetic and minimalist design
  5. Improving uploading systems & Flexibility


Project Management & Design Tools that were used:

  1. Sketch
  2. Trello
  3. Github

To meet these objects each team came up with a plan, as designers and developers we needed to understand the product and the problems that current students were facing. We started the process by researching and sketching ideas which than lead to wire framing and UI development.

As the project was be worked on there were many phases of user & client testing. During these tests new problems and ideas were brought up and implemented before the next sprint review. At the end of the two weeks we had designed and developed a web application for BrainStation that is being used by current students.

Also Working at BrainStaion on numerous development updates, I helped launch their User Experince Design Immersive Program
